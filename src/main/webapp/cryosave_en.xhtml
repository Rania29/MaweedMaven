CORD BLOOD: TOO VALUABLE TO BE WASTED

Your baby’s cord blood is a rich source of powerful stem cells which come from your newborns umbilical cord and can be collected immediately after birth. After the umbilical cord has been clamped and cut, the remaining blood in the umbilical cord is drown into the collection bag by your healthcare provider.

WHAT MAKES CORD BLOOD CELLS DIFFERENT?

Cord blood stem cells are biologically younger and more flexible compared to adult stem cells from other sources like bone marrow. When saved, they have unique qualities and advantages:

  • Collection of cord blood is simple, safe and painless.
  • Preserving them (stops the clock) and protect the cells from aging and being exposed to environmental factors and common viruses that can decrease their functionality.
  • Immediately available and can minimize disease progression in early treatment.
  • Reduces risk of complications when used in transplants.
  • Ability to uses one’s own stem cells for conditions that currently lack medical treatment options, also known as “autologous transplant”.

CORD BLOOD BENEFITS: A GENETIC MATCH

Saving your baby’s blood cord ensures that your baby’s stem cells will be available to your family if you ever need them in future. Families choose private banking because there are significant benefits to use your own stem cells or stem cells from family members if ever needed.

Your baby’s cord blood stem cells and cord tissue may be used by your family in two areas of medicine

  1. Transplant medicines: used to regenerate a healthy blood and immune system, some of which are life-threatening diseases, such as cancer and blood disorders.
  2. Regenerative medicine: is a new field that evaluates stem cells ability to repair and replace cells that been damaged by diseases or injury.
